Related exercises

A new Related exercises section on the piece detail, the multi-select picker that fills it, and the genuinely new piece — the 0–10 score ring (#1009) that retires the 5-step bars everywhere a mastery signal appears.

Decided with you
  • Ring: monochrome arc gauge, numeral centred — 4 treatments below to confirm.
  • Row density: title + key/tempo + ring only. No last-practised / best-tempo clutter.
  • Score 0: empty ring, faint en-dash, never a numeral.
  • Picker: both multi-select patterns drawn (A / B).
Reused, not reinvented

ScreenScaffold, .cardSurface(), HairlineDivider, the type-coded bar (gold = exercise), TypeBadge and the TagFilterSheet chrome all carry over unchanged. The ring reuses the existing score-indigo — no new tokens. The MasteryMeter bars are replaced by the ring on library rows and on the exercise detail.

The one new component · #1009

Score ring · 0–10

Monochrome on cream, numeral centred. Value is encoded twice — by the fill and by the numeral — so it reads with no colour at all. Four treatments at the same score (7) for a fair look; pick one and I'll standardise it.

My pick · A
Arc gauge
Proportional sweep, round cap. Calm, glanceable, scales tiny.
Segmented
Ten ticks = literal 0–10. Countable, very accessible.
Wedge
Pie fill, boldest read. Heavier; less calm at small sizes.
Dial marker
Static ring, serif numeral leads. Calmest, most typographic.

Pressure-testing the ring · Focus player

On the practice focus screen

The focus player already centres on a live timer ring and the rep counter. Related exercises do two things here — they thread into the session queue behind their piece, and the focus screen becomes where a 0–10 score is actually set. The one rule: never show the timer ring and the score ring live at the same time.

Two rings, kept distinct
Timer ring
Gradient, animated, time inside. Lives during play only.
Score ring
Flat monochrome, numeral inside. Lives on rows, detail & summary — set at hand-off.

Where related items travel together · Session builder

Related groups in the session builder

Add a piece and its related exercises come with it as a group — a single block you drag as a unit, so a piece and its related exercises never drift apart. The related exercises sit before the piece; one removal action (swipe or Edit) drops anything you don't want today — it stays related to the piece for next time. The block collapses to one line, and "just the piece" / "ungroup" are always one tap away.
