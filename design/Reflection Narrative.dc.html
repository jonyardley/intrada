Reflection & narrative

Phase 1: four surfaces that turn a session into a story — capture mid-item without leaving the timer, tempo reached at hand-off, structured reflection on the summary, and a session intention set in the builder that echoes at the end. One capture grammar throughout: the sheet, one gesture down.

Calls made
  • Capture = a sheet, never chrome. The player stays bare; the note sheet rises on a swipe and drops back to the running timer. No advance, ever.
  • Tempo = stepper, prefilled at target. A few taps, no keyboard mid-sheet.
  • Reflection lives on the summary, inline and skippable — Save is never gated.
  • Intention is one optional line in the builder, echoed verbatim on the summary.
Flagged, not invented

TempoStepper is the one new primitive candidate — built entirely from existing tokens (hairline border, card surface, 44px targets). If it survives review it goes into Theme.swift + the DS together. The quick-note sheet is the existing BottomSheet at a compact detent — a variant, not a new component. The reflection prompt rows and intention field are composed from shipped inputs and labels. No new colours, no new spacing.

Surface 1 · Focus player

Mid-item quick capture

Three directions weighed: a resident note button in the transport row (rejected — resident chrome on the bare player), a long-press on the item title (rejected — invisible affordance), and the winner: a compact sheet, one swipe up. It reuses the hand-off sheet's grammar, the timer ring stays visible and ticking behind it, and Save or a swipe down returns straight to play. It never advances the item.

Surface 3 · Session summary

Structured reflection, where the evidence is

A post-save interstitial gates the primary action — rejected. Instead the summary itself gains a reflection block: the session intention echoed verbatim, then three prompts — Improved · Still rough · Next target. All optional; blank rows save as nothing. To make the room, the played-ledger collapses to one line (tap expands the shipped rows). Save session stays the only primary action.

Surface 4 · Session builder

One line of intention, before you start

Putting it in the sticky bar competes with Start session, the one primary action; an interstitial after Start is a whole screen for one sentence. The winner: an optional single line under the header — intention-before friction spent exactly where the plan is being made. Whatever is written here is what the summary echoes back.
